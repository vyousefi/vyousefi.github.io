About

My Motivation

I'm fascinated by a simple question with endless depth: what makes a company thrive? My research follows that question into the minds of the executives who set strategy, the salesforce that carries it to market, and the consumers whose experience and well-being ultimately decide whether any of it works. I want to understand how good decisions get made, how technology is rewriting the rules of competition, and how markets can serve people better, not just sell to them more.

That same curiosity shapes how I teach.

My goal in every class is for students to walk out feeling they learned something real. Shape ideas they'll actually use in their work and their lives. I want the material to feel important rather than abstract. Teaching them practical tools for understanding the markets they'll spend their careers in, and the confidence to put them to use.

Dissertation · Marketing Strategy

Executive Cognition and Market-Based Assets: The Influence of CEO Overconfidence on Brand, Social, and Knowledge Equity

Vahid Yousefi & Thomas Brashear Alejandro

Data collection in progress Target: J. of the Academy of Marketing Science Panel Econometrics Event-study DiD Text Analysis

Intangible market-based assets now drive most of a firm's value, yet we know little about how the people at the top shape them. Grounded in Upper Echelons Theory, this project separates CEO overconfidence into overestimation, overplacement, and overprecision, and shows how each builds or erodes a firm's brand, social, and knowledge equity — with CMO presence and CEO emotionality as key moderators. It draws on a large panel of U.S. public firms (ExecuComp, Compustat, Capital IQ, ESG indicators, and earnings-call transcripts), complemented by a matched event-study design.
Stage: data collection is currently in progress.

Marketing Scholarship

You Are Who You Read and Where You Publish: Identifying Your Research Area and Tribe from Thought Leaders

George Milne & Vahid Yousefi

Manuscript in preparation Target: J. of Marketing Education Web Scraping Panel Econometrics Cluster Analysis

This project maps the intellectual structure of marketing scholarship by analyzing the publication and citation records of leading marketing scholars collected from Google Scholar. Using scholars’ publication patterns across core marketing journals, we identify five distinct research “tribes” within the field. The goal is to make the hidden structure of academic marketing more visible, helping PhD students recognize their intellectual community, clarify their research interests, and navigate early career choices more deliberately.

Stage: data has been collected and analyzed; the manuscript is in preparation.

Frontline Employees

A Meta-Analytical Assessment of the Push–Pull–Mooring Theory

Vahid Yousefi, Samira Yaabdollahi & Hossein Hashemi Bahramani

Manuscript in preparation Target: J. of Business Research Meta-Analysis

This project uses meta-analytic methods to synthesize empirical research on push, pull, and mooring factors among salespeople and frontline employees. Drawing on prior studies, we classify the forces that create pressure to leave, attract employees toward alternatives, or keep them attached to their current roles. The project aims to clarify how these forces operate across boundary-spanning work contexts and to identify where the literature offers strong evidence, inconsistent findings, or opportunities for future theory development.

Stage: data has been collected and initial meta-analyses have been conducted; the manuscript direction is currently being refined.

Consumer Behavior

Losing Control Behind the Wheel: How Subscription Models Shape Consumer Ownership and Fairness Perceptions

Samira Yaabdollahi & Vahid Yousefi

Analysis in progress Target: J. of Business Research Experiments

As cars shift from things we own to things we subscribe to, what happens to our sense of ownership — and our sense of fairness? Using experiments, this project tests how subscription-based access reshapes psychological ownership and fairness perceptions in the automotive market.

Stage: data has been collected and analysis is in progress.
