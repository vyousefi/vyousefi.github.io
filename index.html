About

Research that connects strategy and behavior.

My work sits at the meeting point of marketing strategy and behavioral science. On the strategy side, I study how the way executives think — overconfidence, and the over­estimation, over­placement, and over­precision behind it — translates into the market-based assets that drive long-run firm value, using panel econometrics, structural models, machine learning, and text analysis.

On the behavioral side, I examine how consumers make decisions: how they form psychological ownership, judge fairness, and switch between options. Across both, I'm drawn to how businesses actually work and to the role technology plays in shaping decisions on either side of the market.

I expect to complete my PhD in September 2027 and am preparing for the academic job market.
